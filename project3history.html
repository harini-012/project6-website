Year History
1783 John Michell and Pierre-Simon Laplace independently proposed the existence of "dark stars" (precursors to black holes) based on Newton's theory of gravity, suggesting objects with such strong gravity that nothing, not even light, could escape.
1915 Albert Einstein published his theory of General Relativity, which provided the framework for understanding gravity and the potential existence of black holes.
1916 Karl Schwarzschild found the first modern solution to Einstein's equations that described a black hole, now known as the Schwarzschild black hole.
1930 J. Robert Oppenheimer and his students suggested that even neutron stars have a mass limit, beyond which they would collapse into a point where light could not escape, a precursor to the modern description of a black hole.
1964 Early 1960s: Astronomers began discovering quasars, extremely luminous and distant objects, which were later understood as being powered by supermassive black holes.
2015 The Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory (LIGO) detected gravitational waves from a collision between two black holes, providing further evidence for their existence and nature.
2024 Astronomers using the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) discovered a candidate for the oldest-known black hole, dating back to 400 million years after the Big Bang.
