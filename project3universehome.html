Learn How Universe contributes to blackholes



1. Star Evolution and Collapse

  • Massive stars (at least 20 times the Sun’s mass) undergo nuclear fusion, burning hydrogen into heavier elements.
  • Once fusion stops, the core collapses under gravity, leading to a supernova explosion.
  • If the remaining core is above 3 solar masses, it collapses into a black hole due to extreme gravitational pull.

2. Merging of Neutron Stars

  • When two neutron stars (the collapsed remnants of massive stars) merge, their combined mass can exceed the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff limit, forming a black hole.
  • This process is often accompanied by gravitational waves, as detected by LIGO and Virgo observatories.
