Formation of blackholes




1. Stellar Evolution
- A star burns hydrogen into helium through nuclear fusion.
- As fuel depletes, the core contracts under gravity.



2. Supernova Explosion
- In massive stars (≥ 20x Sun’s mass), the core collapses.
- Outer layers explode in a **supernova**, leaving a dense core.



3. Core Collapse
- If the core is **>3 times the Sun’s mass**, gravity overwhelms pressure.
- It collapses into **a singularity** (infinite density, zero volume).

4. Event Horizon Formation
- A **point of no return** forms (event horizon).
- Even light cannot escape beyond this boundary

5. Growth & Accretion
- The black hole **absorbs nearby matter** (gas, dust, stars).
- It grows over time, possibly forming **quasars or merging** with others.
