Essential Black Hole Facts

01
Closest.
The nearest known black hole, called Gaia BH1, is about 1,500 light-years away.

First Point

The nearest known black hole to Earth, named Gaia BH1, lies approximately 1,500 light-years away in the constellation Ophiuchus. This discovery was announced in 2022, based on data collected by the European Space Agency’s Gaia spacecraft, along with follow-up observations using ground-based telescopes.

 02
Farthest.
The most distant black hole detected, at the center of a galaxy called QSO J0313-1806, is around 13 billion light-years away.


Second Point

The most distant black hole discovered so far lies at the center of a galaxy known as QSO J0313–1806, which is located approximately 13 billion light-years from Earth. This supermassive black hole existed just 670 million years after the Big Bang, offering scientists an incredible glimpse into the early universe.

 03
Biggest.
The most massive black hole observed, TON 618, tips the scales at 66 billion times the Sun’s mass.




Third Point

The most massive black hole ever observed is known as TON 618, a ultramassive black hole located about 10.4 billion light-years from Earth. It has an estimated mass of roughly 66 billion times that of the Sun, making it one of the most extreme known objects in the universe.
04
Smallest.
The lightest-known black hole is only 3.8 times the Sun’s mass. It’s paired up with a star.

Fourth Point

The lightest-known black hole discovered so far has a mass of about 3.8 times that of our Sun, which is extremely small by black hole standards. This black hole is part of a binary system, meaning it is paired with a companion star, orbiting around a common center of mass.
